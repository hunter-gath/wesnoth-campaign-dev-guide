Story & dialogue

Both Scenario 04 routes converge in the Oldwood without a separate story panel. Haldric’s party enters under fog and immediately encounters Elilmaldur-Rithrandil, an Ancient Wose who initially treats all humans as habitual tree-cutters. The orcs’ indiscriminate destruction gives Haldric an opportunity to prove that the refugees are “tree-friends.”

After the orc leaders fall, the Wose explains that an earlier human king imprisoned Lich-Lord Lenvan beneath the forest temple. The lich possesses a powerful artifact, which Lady Outlaw identifies as the Ruby of Fire. The Oldwood cannot accompany Haldric beyond its borders, but it can open the magically sealed catacombs.

Map description

The 32 × 32 forest map contains four keeps, extensive deciduous, mixed, pine, and great-tree terrain, swamp pockets, water, villages, roads, and ruined settlement clusters. Haldric and the two orc forces approach a central Wose-held region. The temple at hex 23,22 is visible as a point of interest but remains sealed until the battle is won.

Lessons for campaign authors

Design interpretation. The scenario makes alliance-building the reward structure. Haldric does not recruit Woses afterward; instead, their trust unlocks a location and artifact quest that advances the campaign.
PatternReuse lesson
Allied AI faction with protected leaderCreate cooperation without transferring every allied unit to the player.
Difficulty scales allied help downwardBalance can change the strength of support, not only opposition.
Point of interest shown before it opensForeshadow the next scenario inside the current map.
Clear branch variables at convergencePrevent obsolete path state from leaking into unrelated later logic.
Store a companion for an off-screen taskMake temporary absence preserve level, experience, and identity.
