Final-scenario inversion. The player must control the order and authorship of deaths, not simply maximize kills. Overperforming tactically can invalidate the strategic objective.

Map description

The 52 × 47 map is the campaign’s largest final battlefield. It mixes broad inland plains and forests with marshes, mountains, several island and coastal keeps, shallow and deep water, three destructible bridge systems, and seven staged galleons. Haldric begins in the northeastern interior; Jevyan holds the southeastern sea-castle region near 42,40. Four orc leaders occupy maritime, coastal, island, and northern positions.

Jevyan and the survivor-order logic

  1. The opening upgrades Jevyan Cloaked to the full Jevyan type.
  2. If Jevyan dies while Aethyr remains alive, the scenario ends in defeat.
  3. If Jevyan dies while at least two orc leaders remain, the orcs taunt the player and battle continues.
  4. When only Jevyan and one orc remain, the dying next orc triggers retreat dialogue but does not yet end the level.
  5. Once Jevyan is dead, Aethyr is dead, and exactly one orc remains, the survivor is stored, assigned a boat route, hidden, fake-moved to the coast, and carried away by a Transport Galleon.
  6. If the final orc is killed, the scenario ends in defeat.

Fallen allies rise again

Side-1 deaths, except Aethyr’s, invoke the campaign RISE_UP_RISE_UP macro when the killer does not already supply a corpse through its own plague behavior. The first allied death warns the player that Jevyan is raising the fallen.

Bridge-destruction director

At every new turn, each of three bridge systems is checked independently. A named Cuttle Fish appears and destroys the bridge when:

  • the bridge terrain still exists,
  • a player unit is within radius 8,
  • no player unit is within radius 2, and
  • no unit occupies the bridge itself.

Lessons for campaign authors

PatternReuse lesson
Require a particular killerUse filter_second in last-breath and death events to distinguish valid sacrifice from failure.
Require one enemy survivorQuery living leaders after each death rather than relying on a normal defeat-all objective.
Turn a unit into a physical objectiveAn overlay can show carried story information without a custom unit type.
Destroy terrain conditionallyProximity checks can alter routes only when the change will matter but is not instantly unfair.
Use randomized windows in unlimited scenariosRecurring pressure remains uncertain without a fixed deadline.
