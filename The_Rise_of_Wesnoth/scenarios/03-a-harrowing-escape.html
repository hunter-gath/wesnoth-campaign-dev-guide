Story & dialogue

Haldric enters the mountain pass as the leader of “Refugees,” with Eldaric absent and the orc vanguard already blocking the route. The opening dialogue deliberately leaves little mourning time: Haldric grieves for his father, while Lady Outlaw—if recruited—pushes him back toward action.

A movement event introduces Burin the Lost, a dwarvish steelclad who has been stranded underground for nearly a century. His comic bluntness adds tonal relief while expanding the permanent cast. After both orc leaders fall, Haldric chooses between the river road through the Swamp of Esten and the route through the Midlands.

Map description

The 32 × 42 map is tall and corridor-like, dominated by mountains, hills, narrow roads, forest pockets, water, and bridges. Haldric begins in the upper portion of the pass; the two orc keeps occupy the lower exits. The shape supports a long southward advance rather than a broad front.

Scenario-local macro: ESCAPE_VICTORY

The macro packages the complete branch-selection sequence: dialogue, two menu options, companion reactions, variable assignment, carryover, and a next-scenario override.

Implementation lesson. The scenario declares a default next_scenario, but each [endlevel] inside the choice explicitly overrides it. This keeps a valid default while allowing runtime branching.

Lessons for authors

Design interpretation. The scenario’s goal says “defeat all leaders,” but the experience is structured as a journey: long map geometry, discovery of a companion, signpost foreshadowing, escalation after the first kill, and a destination choice after the second.
PatternReuse lesson
Companion hidden behind movement triggerUse exploration to add party members without making them truly missable if placed near the natural route.
First-kill escalationKeep a two-boss battle tense after one front collapses.
Branch at the victory eventLet the tactical scenario remain shared while allowing strategic route choice.
Companions comment on both optionsUse dialogue to make routes feel like places, not menu labels.
[allow_undo] on flavor interactionsAvoid punishing players for reading non-tactical map content.
