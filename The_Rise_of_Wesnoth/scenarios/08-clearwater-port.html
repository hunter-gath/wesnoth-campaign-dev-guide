Story & dialogue

Clearwater Port is already evacuating its population when Haldric arrives. Commander Aethyr explains that the local army initially drove the orcs back into the Swamp of Esten, but reinforcements destroyed the defense and killed the King of Clearwater. Three more shiploads are needed before the remaining population can escape.

The opening conversation formalizes the Wesfolk as Haldric’s allies, introduces Aethyr as the local commander, and turns the scenario into a moral timing choice. The player may leave early, remain for a later ship, or fight until every enemy leader is dead. The longer the party remains, the more heroic—and mechanically rewarding—the outcome becomes.

Map description

The 42 × 37 winter coastal map stretches from mountains and frozen inland approaches in the west to Clearwater’s harbor and open sea in the east. Haldric enters near the city, Aethyr’s forward castle and inner wall guard the eastern settlement, and three orc armies attack from the landward side. Bridges, shallow water, deep water, snow, hills, roads, villages, city terrain, and a lighthouse create several distinct defensive layers.

Commander Aethyr’s stateful allied AI

Side 2 is reconfigured every one of its turns. The scenario records its posture in previous_ai_position and can move among four broad modes:

FORWARD DEFENSE ├── player pushes far west ─────────────► OFFENSIVE SUPPORT └── 10+ orcs swarm forward castle ├── first withdrawal turn ───────► RETREAT FIRSTTURN ├── second withdrawal turn ──────► RETREAT SECONDTURN └── withdrawal completed ────────► CITY-WALL DEFENSE
ModeImplementation behavior
Forward defenseResets defensive AI, assigns high-value goal hexes in the outer castle, and avoids overextension.
Full retreatUses extreme caution, disables ordinary attacking, and moves troops toward the inner city.
Retreat attack workaroundTemporarily moves non-leader units to hidden side 3 for one turn so adjacent enemies can still be attacked without allowing forward movement.
City-wall defenseAssigns weighted goal hexes along the wall and avoids advancing back into the field.
Offensive supportIf at least four player units are far forward, resets side 2 to an offensive posture so it can support them.

Dynamic allied recruitment

Aethyr normally recruits inexpensive Peasants, Bowmen, and Spearmen. Peasants are limited to two so the AI does not repeatedly expose fragile units along the wall. At the start of each side-2 turn, the scenario checks the garrison’s stored gold:

Gold stateExtra recruits
60 or lessNone
More than 60Heavy Infantryman, Longbowman, Javelineer, Pikeman, Swordsman

This allows the AI to spend accumulated resources on advanced defenders when the player has not captured its villages.

Lessons for campaign authors

Design interpretation. The scenario turns time into an ethical difficulty selector. The player chooses when enough has been sacrificed, while the allied AI makes the city feel defended by an actual garrison rather than decorative units.
PatternReuse lesson
Graded victoriesOffer multiple valid exits with narrative labels and different rewards.
Stateful allied AITrack posture explicitly when an ally must perform recognizable military phases.
Dynamic recruit qualityLet accumulated AI gold unlock stronger units instead of sitting unused.
Schedule-driven physical transportAnimate arrivals and departures so the turn deadline exists on the map.
Refactor repeated exits into a macroKeep three boarding interactions consistent and easy to rebalance.
Unlock a humble recruit through storyThe new Peasant recruit records the port farmers’ support.
