Story and dialogue

Several months pass while Jessene translates the Book and Haldric experiments with the Ruby. The scenario opens by turning the artifact from a plot object into a permanent attack, then immediately interrupts the experiment with news that the orcs have seized Southbay’s frontier.

Turn 4 reveals the larger antagonist: Lich-Lord Jevyan arrives with his Familiar and Addroran’s undead son, the Fool Prince. Jevyan announces his alliance with the orcs and senses the Ruby. Turn 5 brings Lord Typhon, whose earlier agreement with the elder prince becomes a new alliance with Haldric’s refugees. Merfolk recruitment is unlocked, linking sea travel, diplomacy, and roster progression.

Map description

The 47 × 47 map is the campaign’s largest documented battlefield so far. Southbay’s city and harbor occupy the central-southern region, with northern bridge castles, a river defense, western coast and lighthouse, multiple galleons, roads to the Midlands and Clearwater, and broad eastern and northern frontier terrain.

King Addroran’s allied AI

Side 2 is reset every turn. Unlike Clearwater’s four-state variable machine, this scenario derives its posture directly from unit positions:

ConditionResponse
Northern bridge not overrunDefend two northern castle positions and the main city walls.
At least five enemy units near the bridge, fewer than two Southbay units there, and no player units supporting itAvoid the north and reposition toward weighted southern-riverbank goals.
All conditionsAlways maintain weighted goal locations along the main wall.

The scenario also removes the leader_shares_keep candidate action, limits Addroran to one move, and gives him shallow-water movement cost 1 so he can reach a nearby healing oasis if poisoned.

The object is embedded directly in the newly created hero rather than placed as a pickup, guaranteeing that Typhon always arrives with his signature weapon.

Jevyan’s scripted intelligence visit

Jevyan’s party does not behave like a conventional enemy leader. The hidden side exists for a scripted dramatic entrance:

  1. Familiar flies in as a messenger and observer.
  2. Jevyan Cloaked enters, speaks, and senses the Ruby.
  3. The Fool Prince is shown as a Death Knight.
  4. Jevyan leaves the map.
  5. Revenant and Bone Shooter remain, with more undead on higher difficulties.
  6. Familiar also departs.

This separates antagonist exposition from normal recruitment and pathfinding.

Lessons for campaign authors

PatternReuse lesson
Translate an artifact across several scenarios before activating itDelay mechanical payoff to make the item feel researched and consequential.
Permanent object on a heroAdd a new attack without creating another unit type or advancement branch.
Roster unlock through returning diplomacyMake an earlier worldbuilding agreement produce a later recruit list.
Position-derived allied AIA posture variable is unnecessary when current battlefield state is sufficient.
Script the villain’s visit separately from battle AIGuarantee timing, dialogue, and exit while leaving a smaller combat consequence.
