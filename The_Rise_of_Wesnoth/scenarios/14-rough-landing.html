Story and dialogue

A severe storm damages the Eldaric and scatters much of the refugee fleet. Lord Typhon recommends a group of small windswept islands where the ships can regroup and make landfall. The islands are Naga scavenging territory, however, and the local leaders immediately treat the fleet as prey.

The victory dialogue restores the campaign’s forward motion: the fleet regroups, the next landfall should be the Great Continent, and Jevyan’s Familiar is seen following the refugees.

Map description

The compact 27 × 32 map consists largely of deep ocean and shallow water surrounding several small sandy, scrub-covered islands. Haldric’s red galleon is placed near the southern landing area. The three Naga keeps are distributed across separate island positions, forcing the player to use coastal and aquatic movement rather than a single land front.

Casualty-triggered Sea Serpents

The first casualty belonging to each enemy side schedules one named Sea Serpent for that side. A global delay counter spaces the three scheduled arrivals by one, two, and three turns in the order the sides first suffer a casualty.

Originating sideSerpent nameMessage
2Chompey the SerpentGrowling roar
3Scaly the SerpentRoar
4Toothey the SerpentHiss

The nested new turn event waits until its scheduled turn and searches deep-ocean hexes that:

  • contain no unit,
  • are within radius 10 of a side-1 unit, and
  • are not within radius 9 of a side-1 unit.

The resulting ring places the serpent exactly ten hexes from the nearest player unit, keeping the arrival visible and relevant without allowing an immediate one-turn attack.

Lessons for campaign authors

PatternReuse lesson
Make casualties schedule later consequencesA battle can escalate because of progress without spawning everything immediately.
Place arrivals in a distance ringKeep reinforcements relevant while avoiding unfair immediate contact.
Use the newly unlocked faction immediatelyAn ocean map validates the value of merfolk recruitment from Scenario 12.
Keep surveillance as a short victory beatMaintain antagonist continuity without changing the current objective.
