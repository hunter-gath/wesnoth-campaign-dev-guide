Story & dialogue

The refugees enter Southbay through an abandoned underground riverbed converted into a sewer. The twin Red Wizards—Daellyn and Tinry—were banished below after the Wesfolk War and can sense the Ruby of Fire. Their sudden appearance continues the campaign’s pattern that powerful magical beings detect the artifact.

Jessene explicitly connects the Ancient Wose, Caror, and the twins through this magical sensitivity. The scenario’s story is otherwise environmental: refuse, blocked channels, rubble, spiders, bats, scorpions, shroud, and the narrow exit all turn a shortcut into a dangerous urban underworld.

Map description

The 37 × 27 underground map is horizontally oriented. Haldric begins on the eastern side and must reach the far-west Southbay exit. Cave walls, old river channels, sewer water, fungus, rubble, bridges, village chambers, and narrow corridors split the route around a blocked central streambed.

Twin-boss reinforcement pattern

Each wizard’s death event checks whether his brother is still alive:

FIRST TWIN DIES └── surviving brother says "Brother!" └── surviving side receives 120 gold SECOND TWIN DIES └── killer discovers a map and gold ├── player receives 50 gold └── large route area is unshrouded
Design effect. The first success makes the remaining boss stronger; the second success repays the player with information and resources.

Lessons for campaign authors

PatternReuse lesson
Escape objective with optional bossesLet players choose safety or extra reward without branching the next scenario.
First boss death empowers the survivorPrevent a symmetric two-leader battle from collapsing after one victory.
Second boss death reveals navigationUse optional combat to reduce exploration uncertainty.
Same exit, conditional bonusKeep map language simple while varying reward logic in the exit event.
Fixed guardians plus recruited enemiesUse different systems for corridor encounters and strategic armies.
