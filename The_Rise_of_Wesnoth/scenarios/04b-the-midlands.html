Story & dialogue

The shared first panel again closes the Stormvale chapter. The route-specific panel presents the Midlands as formerly prosperous hill-and-forest kingdoms—the Green Isle’s breadbasket and a supplier of lumber and gemstones—now being destroyed by the orc invasion.

The opening dialogue is immediate and public rather than eerie: smoke is visible, Haldric condemns the sack, and the orc leaders joke about unexpectedly finding action behind the front. At victory, Sir Ladoc reports that the party has encountered only the orc rear guard and that the main army is moving on Southbay. As in the river route, the road ahead is too dangerous and Haldric turns toward Oldwood.

Map description

The 42 × 40 map is broader and more open than the swamp. It mixes grassland, dry grass, hills, deciduous and mixed forest, roads, villages, shallow water, swamp pockets, and burned/ruined settlement terrain. Haldric’s keep lies in the upper-right half, while the three orc leaders occupy separated keeps toward the left and lower portions. Two signposts point toward Southbay and Oldwood.

Lessons for campaign authors

Design interpretation. The Midlands route is a pressure-and-response scenario. The player sees the enemies and chooses fronts openly, while scripted reserves and survivor funding prevent success on one front from making the rest trivial.
PatternReuse lesson
Reinforcement triggered by attacking a leaderMake a boss feel defended without spawning units before they matter.
Different reserve compositions per frontGive geographically similar objectives distinct tactical identities.
Reinforce the surviving enemyMaintain difficulty after a multi-leader battle begins to collapse.
Map art tells civilian historyRuins and burned terrain can carry exposition silently.
Alternate branch with same convergenceOffer meaningful variety without doubling the entire later campaign.
