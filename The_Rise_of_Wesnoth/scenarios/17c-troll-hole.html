Story and premise

An elven guide leads Haldric into the Brown Hills and down a large cave mouth. Burin supplies the essential tactical warning: Trolls are strong, hostile, and regenerate rapidly. The elves have assigned a direct extermination task, and Haldric later recognizes that they delegated a particularly unpleasant job.

Map description

The 32 × 32 shrouded cave is broadly square and organized around four outer corner regions, a central underground-water complex, narrow cave passages, villages, fungus, rubble, and impassable walls. Four Troll sides are defined, but only two leaders remain after randomized prestart setup. The other two corners receive Giant Spider guardians named Fang.

Randomized corner layout

A random value from 1 to 4 removes two adjacent Troll leaders and replaces their corners with Giant Spiders. The remaining two Troll Warriors are the scenario’s actual leaders.

Random resultLeaders removedSurviving leaders
1Erart and RaolRilg and Gulg
2Raol and RilgErart and Gulg
3Rilg and GulgErart and Raol
4Gulg and ErartRaol and Rilg

Each removed leader is replaced by a Giant Spider named Fang on another Troll side, retaining hostility without keeping all four recruitment centers active.

Lessons for campaign authors

PatternReuse lesson
Randomize which leaders exist, not the entire mapGain replay variation while preserving tested geography.
Replace removed leaders with guardiansKeep every corner tactically relevant.
Use shroud to protect the random revealPrevent pre-battle inspection from solving the layout immediately.
Append traits to macro-generated unitsUse additive WML tags when the source macro does not expose every desired field.
Scale regeneration-heavy foes through economy and aggressionDifficulty need not require custom Troll mechanics.
