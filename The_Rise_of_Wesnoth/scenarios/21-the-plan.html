The deception plan

Haldric proposes convincing Jevyan and the surviving orcs that the refugees traded the Ruby of Fire to the elves in exchange for settlement rights. A forged treaty must be found on a trusted human’s corpse, Jevyan must believe it, and at least one literate-enough witness must return to the Green Isle with the false story.

Haldric initially intends to sacrifice himself. Jessene refuses, because his survival is necessary to lead the refugees. Commander Aethyr emerges from hiding and insists on taking Haldric’s place, framing the mission as the honorable death denied to him at Clearwater Port and during the fleet rescue.

Map and staging

The 22 × 12 coastal clearing is a compact conversation stage of grassland, deciduous forest, shallow and deep water, marsh, villages, sand, and two bridges. Haldric, Jessene, and Aethyr are positioned in a triangular arrangement. Aethyr begins hidden and enters midway through the argument.

Safety restoration

prestart recalls Lady Jessene. If she is somehow absent—described in the source as impossible under normal play—the file restores her from jessica_store, places her at 8,5, and clears the variable. This makes the cutscene robust against an unexpected prior state.

Aethyr’s entrance technique

  1. Aethyr is recalled and teleported to 14,10.
  2. The unit is hidden.
  3. When he interrupts, the scenario stores his type without killing him.
  4. move_unit_fake animates his approach.
  5. The temporary type store is cleared.
  6. The real Aethyr is teleported, unhidden, and begins speaking.

This separates visual entrance staging from the persistent unit’s real movement.
