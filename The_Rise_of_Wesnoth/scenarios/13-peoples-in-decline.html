Story and dialogue

The refugees leave the Green Isle permanently aboard the Eldaric. The story panels describe a stormy eastern voyage and a slowly sinking volcanic island where Southbay’s elder prince once established a foothold among belligerent drakes.

The port appears deserted, but Jessene recognizes the island and warns that its inhabitants have rearmed. Haldric attempts peaceful resupply; the drakes answer in the name of Morogor. After the battle, the refugees replenish the fleet, while Jessene notes that the drakes will resume guarding the island and eventually face evacuation themselves.

Map description

The 32 × 33 map is almost entirely surrounded by deep ocean. A compact central island combines beaches, sand, scrub, swamp, tropical forest, villages, rough roads, caves, volcanic terrain, and three drake keeps. Haldric’s red galleon is staged at the crude port near the upper-left interior.

Unlike Typhon’s guaranteed trident in Scenario 12, this is a normal optional pickup. The same core effect is therefore demonstrated in two authoring contexts.

Bitey the Serpent event

The first side-1 unit to enter the trident hex schedules a nested new turn event. On the following turn:

  1. Random x and y coordinates are selected near the item.
  2. A named loyal Sea Serpent, “Bitey the Serpent,” is created for hidden side 5.
  3. A nearby side-1 unit warns the player.
  4. The camera scrolls to Bitey.
  5. The temporary coordinate variables are cleared.

Delaying the spawn until the next turn prevents the creature from interrupting the pickup interaction itself.

Lessons for campaign authors

Design interpretation. The first overseas battle introduces a new faction and environment without introducing new campaign-specific units. Its custom identity comes from the sinking-island story, map composition, and a small optional encounter around a core item.
PatternReuse lesson
Resupply as a victory premiseGive a conventional leader-defeat battle logistical stakes.
Use another displaced people as thematic mirrorMake a new faction reinforce the campaign’s central theme.
Core item plus scripted guardianTurn an existing pickup into a scenario-specific encounter.
Three similar enemy sides with distinct advanced recruitsCreate subtle tactical differences without custom AI.
Villain surveillance after victoryMaintain antagonist continuity without interrupting the current battle.
