Story & dialogue

Summer gives way to fall, Eldaric fortifies the frontier, and the orc invasion reaches Stormvale. The opening scene immediately reframes the campaign: the human kingdom can no longer defend its home, the only escape is the southern pass, and the Wesfolk occupy that pass.

The scenario’s climax is not merely the Wesfolk leader’s defeat. Haldric must decide whether to accept her surrender and skills or reject her. Accepting creates Lady Outlaw as a loyal hero and grants access to outlaw recruits. Rejecting grants an immediate gold reward while allowing her to escape for later use. Eldaric then remains behind to cover the refugees’ retreat.

Map description

The map is a 37 × 27 return to the Stormvale region, now configured as a four-side crisis. Orc leaders occupy northern and northeastern keeps, Haldric begins around the southeastern settlement, and the Wesfolk block the southern escape route. A central water system, bridges, roads, swamp, and mountain belts shape movement between these fronts.

Local macro: NORTH_GUARD

The scenario defines a short helper macro to create named loyal guards. On Easy they are Shock Troopers; on Normal and Hard they are Heavy Infantrymen. Parameters supply coordinates, ID, display name, and a second trait.

#define NORTH_GUARD X Y ID_STRING NAME_STRING SECOND_TRAIT_WML
    [unit]
        type=(difficulty-dependent heavy infantry)
        side=1
        id={ID_STRING}
        x={X}
        y={Y}
        [modifications]
            {TRAIT_LOYAL}
            {SECOND_TRAIT_WML}
        [/modifications]
    [/unit]
#enddef

This is a good example of a scenario-local abstraction: it removes repetition without promoting a one-off pattern into the campaign-wide utility layer.

Lessons for authors

Design interpretation. The scenario creates urgency through objective competition rather than a special countdown mechanic: the player must reach the Wesfolk before the orcs do, while optional loot rewards risky counterattacks.
