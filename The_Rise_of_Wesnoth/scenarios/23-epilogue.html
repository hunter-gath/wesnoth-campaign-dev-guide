Map reuse and memorial staging

The same 22 × 12 clearing used to plan Aethyr’s sacrifice becomes his funeral site. prestart changes hex 10,5 to road terrain and places items/flower2.png there as a memorial. The reuse gives the location a before-and-after meaning: first resolve, then remembrance.

Haldric is stored with kill=yes, fake-moved into the ceremony using his current advancement type, restored at 11,7, and the temporary haldric_s variable is cleared.

Funeral and the secret

Minister Edren conducts Aethyr’s funeral. Haldric credits the sacrifice with the victory and remembers Eldaric, Addroran, and the many dead. If any optional long-term companion remains—Edren, Burin, Ruddry, or Ladoc—Haldric asks for an oath of secrecy and reveals that the elves never received the Ruby.

Ruby and political aftermath

Jessene confirms that the Ruby remains hidden in the Troll Hole. Haldric decides to aid the elves against the orcs who failed to escape, partly to honor the pact and partly because the refugees depend on elven support during their first winter.

The artifact is not restored to Haldric’s attack list in the epilogue. The campaign closes with the Ruby concealed rather than returned to active use.

Naming Wesnoth

Jessene derives the kingdom’s name from the old language:

Final nameWesnoth

Haldric accepts the title of first King of Wesnoth. He asks Jessene to remain beside him; she accepts, and the epilogue describes their descendants as mingling the two refugee ancestries.

Legend versus remembered history

The narration distinguishes the public epic, “The Breaking of the Pact,” from the fuller history shown by the campaign. Later generations remember Haldric’s betrayal of the elves but not all of the elves’ private intentions or the refugees’ desperation. The final narrator frames the campaign as the preserved true story of the kingdom’s rise.

Lessons for campaign authors

PatternReuse lesson
Reuse the planning map for a funeralReturn to a location after the consequence of the decision made there.
Condition companion futures on survivalGive optional long-lived units individual conclusions without requiring separate epilogues.
Let mechanics remain unresolved when the story demands itThe Ruby attack stays removed because the artifact remains hidden.
Close personal, political, and historiographic arcs togetherThe marriage, kingdom, institutions, and later legend all emerge from prior scenario state.
