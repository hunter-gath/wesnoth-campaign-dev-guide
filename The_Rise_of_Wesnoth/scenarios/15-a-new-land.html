Story and dialogue

After the fleet finally reaches the Great Continent, the refugees land in the middle of an elven–dwarven territorial dispute. The elves accuse dwarven colonists of violating an ancient treaty; Dursil argues that the dwarves have outgrown their northern mines and rejects elven control over southern forest resources.

The arrival of many human ships temporarily unites the continental factions against the refugees. Haldric repeatedly attempts his practiced peace speech, but the situation becomes violent before diplomacy can begin. The first death brings Lady Dionli, who stops the battle, recognizes the newcomers as kin to the earlier Prince of Southbay, orders the dwarves north, and summons Haldric to the Ka’lian.

Map description

The 41 × 42 coastal map places the refugee landing among ocean, beaches, grassland, forest, villages, roads, hills, mountains, water channels, and three continental keeps. Glimir and Eowarar command elven forces while Dursil commands dwarves. Their shared team makes them temporary allies against Haldric despite their opening argument.

Map systemDesign role
Large coast and visible fleetMakes the locals’ invasion interpretation understandable.
Three continental recruitment centersCreates pressure from distinct elven and dwarven rosters.

Objectives and unusual reward model

Success conditionAvoid deaths for as long as possible; the scenario ends when any ordinary unit dies.
DefeatDeath of Haldric, Lady Jessene, or Lord Typhon.
Turn limitUnlimited while no death occurs.
Carryover20% of finishing gold.
BonusCalculated to equal an early-finish bonus for the number of turns survived.

When the terminating death occurs, the scenario sets the turn limit to the current turn and then adds the current turn again. The resulting total is twice the survived turn count, leaving an equal number of “remaining” turns for Wesnoth’s early-finish calculation.

Design inversion. Ordinary campaigns reward finishing faster. This scenario uses the same engine bonus system to reward surviving longer without a death.

Custom Lua AI: aggressive attack without suicide

Each continental side replaces the normal combat, high-XP attack, and poison-spreading candidate actions with ca_aggressive_attack_no_suicide.lua. The scenario’s objective note explicitly tells the player that enemies will not initiate an attack if they risk dying.

The candidate action:

  1. Gets every unit able to attack.
  2. Enumerates possible attacks, including occupied destinations.
  3. Simulates each combat.
  4. Rejects any attack where the attacker has a nonzero chance of death.
  5. Rates the survivors by defender kill chance, expected defender damage, low expected attacker damage, and stronger attacker HP.
  6. Moves and attacks with the highest-rated option.
Rating componentEffect
Defender death chance × 100Strongly favors possible kills.
Expected defender damageRewards useful nonlethal attacks.
Expected attacker damage ÷ 10Penalizes self-harm.
Attacker HP ÷ 10Breaks ties toward stronger attackers.

Escalating income

At every new turn, sides 2–4 receive base income equal to $turn_number - 1. The scenario therefore becomes progressively harder even though it has no fixed deadline.
