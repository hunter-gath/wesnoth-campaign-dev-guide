Story and dialogue

Haldric emerges from Southbay’s sewer and gains an audience with King Addroran IX. The scene resolves several long-running political questions: whether Haldric is already a king, whether the Wesfolk will be admitted into Southbay, why the refugees must flee east rather than attempt to retake the Green Isle, and who will lead the evacuation.

Jessene reveals that she can translate the Book of Fire and Darkness, that she served as navigator and companion to Addroran’s elder son, and that the Wesfolk deliberately came from the overcrowded and monster-filled west. Her people’s central historical failure was waiting until their cities had already fallen before migrating. Addroran accepts the lesson, pledges Southbay as a winter refuge, and chooses to remain behind while Haldric and Jessene lead the young and able-bodied east.

Map and staging

The 23 × 35 winter map is a compact coastal-city stage rather than a tactical battlefield. It contains snow and ice, coastal water, roads, urban castle terrain, bridges, villages, hills, forest, a lighthouse, the opened sewer trapdoor, a signpost, and two transport galleons.

Staging elementPurpose
Lighthouse with aura at 3,5Maintains continuity with Southbay’s coastal identity.
Open trapdoor at 16,17Shows the party’s physical arrival from Scenario 10.
Two galleonsForeshadow evacuation logistics before the fleet is ready.
Addroran as side-2 leaderAllows the conversation to occur among actual map units.

There are no objectives and no shared death macro because the player never receives a tactical turn.

Lessons for campaign authors

Design interpretation. The scenario does no tactical work, but it performs major campaign work: legitimizing an alliance, defining migration policy, assigning leadership, motivating a final defense, and initiating artifact translation.
PatternReuse lesson
Political cutscene on a real mapUse environmental staging when place and logistics matter.
Knowledge-for-rights negotiationLet story agreements change faction relationships rather than only dialogue tone.
100% carryover through non-combat chaptersAvoid taxing the player for narrative interludes.
Explicit generational evacuationMake the campaign’s population-scale stakes concrete.
