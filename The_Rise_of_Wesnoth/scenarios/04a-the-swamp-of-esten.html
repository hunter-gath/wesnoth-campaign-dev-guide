Story & dialogue

The shared first story panel closes the Stormvale arc by emphasizing Eldaric’s sacrifice and Haldric’s permanent exile. The route-specific panel explains that the river road leads toward Clearwater Port through land polluted during the Wesfolk War and now occupied by an undead-imitation cult.

The playable opening establishes a deliberately uneasy atmosphere. Fog conceals the swamp, Haldric remembers that local forces once cleared it twice yearly, Burin complains about the smell, and Lady Outlaw senses an ambush. Victory brings Sir Ruddry, sole survivor of a Clearwater Port expeditionary force, who warns that the direct river road is blocked by an immense orc army. Haldric therefore redirects the refugees through Oldwood.

Map description

The 37 × 42 map is a long wetland journey composed primarily of swamp, shallow and deep water, wooded grassland, rough roads, hills, villages, and scattered castle/keep structures. Haldric begins near a castle in the upper-left portion, while the three cult leaders occupy separated positions across the map. Five temple images and one eastern signpost turn otherwise ordinary hexes into exploration nodes.

Visibility-aware scorpion ambush

The scenario’s most advanced system is not AI code; it is an event-driven ambush algorithm:

  1. A side-1 unit enters within radius 4 of one of three centers.
  2. The region is added to triggered_scorpion_locs so it cannot trigger again.
  3. A nested new turn event searches terrain at the boundary between visible and unseen hexes.
  4. The search starts at radius 5 around the triggering unit and expands until a valid location is found or the radius reaches 20.
  5. A loyal Giant Scorpion for side 2 is animated into that location.
  6. Search variables are cleared.
Why this is notable. The ambush appears near the player’s current vision edge rather than at a fixed coordinate. The same authored event therefore adapts to different movement paths.

Randomized temple system

Three local macros define temple outcomes:

MacroOutcome
TEMPLE_HELP X YCreates Minister Edren and recruitment-style dialogue.
TEMPLE_TRAP X YSpawns hostile units belonging to multiple cult sides.
TEMPLE_LOOT X YGrants 50 gold.

A random value from 1 to 3 assigns help, trap, and loot among three temples. The temple at 34,36 is always a trap; the temple at 30,10 is always loot. This mixes reliable knowledge with replay variation.

Lessons for campaign authors

Design interpretation. The swamp route makes uncertainty the central mechanic. Fog, randomized temples, distributed leaders, and adaptive ambush placement all reinforce the fiction that the region has not been safely patrolled.
PatternReuse lesson
Adaptive spawn at vision edgeUse player perception, not only coordinates, to place fair-feeling ambushes.
Randomized points of interestGive repeat players uncertainty while preserving some fixed anchors.
One scenario, several exploration rewardsOptional companions, loot, and traps make detours meaningful.
Difficulty changes unit tier, not merely quantityHigher levels can alter tactical texture, not just numbers.
Temporary variables explicitly clearedKeep save-state hygiene visible in complex event scripting.
