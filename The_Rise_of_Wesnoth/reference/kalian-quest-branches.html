The hub uses the flag to hide the completed option, last_done to select the return greeting, and num_done to choose the council’s overall progress response.

Four approaches to variation

Authored phase change

The Dragon: a hidden boss appears because of space or time.

Authored reinforcement threshold

Lizard Beach: crossing the beach line creates a new allied enemy force.

Randomized strategic layout

Troll Hole: random setup changes which corners recruit.

Randomized exploration identity

Cursed Isle: fixed temples receive different encounter roles.

Economy and reward comparison

Authoring-cost comparison

BranchFresh campaign-specific productionMajor reused content
DragonMap, WML state and dialogueFire Dragon, complete Saurian roster, swamp terrain, music
BeachMap, dialogue and reinforcement eventSaurian/Naga units, coast terrain, music
Troll HoleMap, random setup and macro compositionTrolls, spiders, tentacles, cave terrain, music
Cursed IsleMap, randomized temples and Queen scriptingSpectres, Vampire Lady, bats, holy-water macro, island terrain, music
Production lesson. Four highly distinct branches require no new ordinary unit type. Variety comes primarily from map design, event architecture, objective logic, faction selection, and state contracts.
