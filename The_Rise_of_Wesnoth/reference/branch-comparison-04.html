Shared branch structure

03 A Harrowing Escape │ ├── escape_choice = 1 ──► 04a The Swamp of Esten ──┐ │ │ └── escape_choice = 2 ──► 04b The Midlands ─────────┤ ▼ 05 The Oldwood

Both branches use the same protagonist level, starting gold, default schedule, primary/extra gameplay music, 40% carryover, shared exile story panel, recalled companions, victory objective, winter deadline, and final strategic conclusion. Their scenario-specific systems are intentionally very different.

Branching-effort analysis

Design interpretation. These branches are expensive where the player notices—map, faction, encounters, dialogue, and tactical rhythm—but economical in campaign architecture. They share the entry point, core cast, balance frame, and convergence point.
Authoring strategyBenefit
Share campaign-state assumptionsBoth files can recall the same companions and inherit the same recruit list.
Use a common ending destinationLater scenarios do not need two complete versions.
Give each route one signature systemThe choice feels meaningful without requiring different core rules.
Record the completed route for the transitionTransition or journey logic can distinguish the completed branch before Oldwood clears the marker.
Introduce route-specific loyal unitsThe branch leaves a durable mechanical souvenir.
