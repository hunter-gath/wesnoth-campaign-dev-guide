Pacing architecture

05 — Social proof through combat

The player earns trust by defending a faction with its own army and leader.

06 — Focused dungeon objective

The campaign narrows from a multi-front war to one enemy and one artifact.

07 — Recovery and redirection

A short non-combat chapter processes consequences before the next siege.

Design interpretation. The arc alternates tactical scale rather than escalating monotonically. A large alliance battle is followed by a compact dungeon and then a pure narrative scene.

Best practices demonstrated

PracticeOldwood example
Make allies mechanically independentTreefolk retain their own side, recruitment, gold, and AI.
Gate an objective behind story-earned accessThe catacomb cannot be entered until the Woses break the seal.
Let objectives complete in either order when naturalLenvan and the Ruby can be handled in either sequence.
Use temporary state for local logicHave_Ruby is discarded once success guarantees possession.
Use stored units for long-lived progressionlady_store retains experience and type through absence.
Give narrative scenarios real engineering responsibilitiesScenario 07 performs identity, roster, variable, and carryover transitions.
Reuse maps when place continuity mattersOldwood is revisited rather than represented by a generic story screen.
